What is Biomedical Engineering?

Biomedical engineering is an interdisciplinary field that combines principles of engineering with biological and medical sciences to design and create equipment, devices, computer systems, and software used in healthcare. In Kenya, this field is rapidly growing as the country seeks to improve its healthcare infrastructure and develop local solutions for medical challenges.

Biomedical engineers in Kenya work on diverse projects ranging from developing affordable medical devices to improving hospital information systems and creating prosthetics using locally available materials.

Education and Universities

Leading Institutions Offering Biomedical Engineering

University of Nairobi
Offers a Bachelor of Science in Biomedical Engineering through the School of Engineering. The program focuses on medical device design, biomechanics, and healthcare technology systems.
Kenyatta University
Provides biomedical engineering courses integrated within their engineering and medical programs, with emphasis on practical applications in Kenyan healthcare settings.
Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology (JKUAT)
Offers specialized courses in biomedical engineering with strong research components focused on agricultural and rural healthcare applications.
Technical University of Kenya
Provides diploma and degree programs in biomedical engineering technology, preparing students for hands-on technical roles in healthcare facilities.

Typical Curriculum Components

  • Mathematics and Physics fundamentals
  • Biology and Human Anatomy
  • Engineering principles (mechanical, electrical, computer)
  • Medical device design and development
  • Biomaterials and tissue engineering
  • Healthcare information systems
  • Ethics in medical technology
  • Project-based learning with real healthcare challenges

Typical Job Roles

Clinical Engineer
Manages and maintains medical equipment in healthcare facilities, ensures safety compliance, and trains medical staff on equipment use.
Medical Device Developer
Designs and develops new medical devices, particularly focusing on affordable solutions for resource-limited settings.
Healthcare IT Specialist
Develops and implements health information systems, electronic medical records, and telemedicine platforms.
Research Scientist
Conducts research in areas such as biomaterials, medical imaging, or rehabilitation engineering.

Challenges and Solutions

Current Challenges

Limited Local Manufacturing
Most medical devices are imported, creating dependency and high costs. Solution: Government incentives for local manufacturing and technology transfer programs.
Skills Gap
Shortage of qualified biomedical engineers and technicians. Solution: Expanded training programs and international collaboration for knowledge transfer.
Funding Constraints
Limited funding for research and development in biomedical engineering. Solution: Public-private partnerships and international development funding.
Regulatory Framework
Need for stronger regulatory oversight of medical devices. Solution: Strengthening the Pharmacy and Poisons Board's capacity for medical device regulation.

Emerging Solutions

The biomedical engineering community in Kenya is addressing these challenges through:

  • Collaborative research projects with international universities
  • Development of low-cost, locally appropriate medical technologies
  • Training programs for biomedical technicians
  • Advocacy for supportive policy frameworks
